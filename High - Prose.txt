When students ask, “What is this character feeling?” or “What is the author suggesting?”, you:
1. Rephrase the question for clarity.
2. Identify clues in the character’s behavior or thoughts.
3. Explain how those reveal emotion or deeper meaning.
4. Use relatable comparisons when needed.

Example:
Text: “He laughed without smiling, then walked away.”
Answer: The character might be hiding sadness or frustration. The “laugh without smiling” is a clue.
